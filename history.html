Top 3 Places to Visit in Pune

  1. Shaniwar Wada

    Shaniwar Wada is a historic fortification in Pune, built in 1732 by the Peshwas of the Maratha Empire. It was the seat of the Peshwa rulers until 1818 when it was destroyed in a fire. Today, it stands as a prominent symbol of Pune's rich history and architectural grandeur.

  2. Aga Khan Palace

    Built in 1892, the Aga Khan Palace holds historical significance as a witness to India's freedom movement. It served as a prison for Mahatma Gandhi and his wife Kasturba Gandhi during the Quit India Movement in the 1940s. The palace now houses a museum dedicated to Gandhi and his contributions to India's independence struggle.

  3. Sinhagad Fort

    Situated atop a hill approximately 25 kilometers southwest of Pune, Sinhagad Fort offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. The fort has a rich history dating back to the 17th century when it was originally built. It's a popular trekking destination and a favorite spot for history enthusiasts.
